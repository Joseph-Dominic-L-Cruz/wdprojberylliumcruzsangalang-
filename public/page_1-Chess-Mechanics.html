Rules in the Game of Chess

Understanding Each Piece in Chess
King

The king is a piece that can move one square in any direction and can capture pieces along its path. However, the king cannot move in squares wherein other pieces can capture it. In the case that a king lies along the path of another pieces such that it can be eaten, the king is in a "check" status, and the player must move the king away from the said path and from any other paths wherein the piece can be captured. In the case that a player's king can be captured and that the king has no legal way to escape, the king is in a "checkmate" status, and the other player who placed the king in such state emerges victorious in the game.
